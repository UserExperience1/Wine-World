Petit Verdot – Depth, color intensity, and spice

Vermentino, found along the coasts of Sardinia and Liguria, delights with its zesty citrus, herbal notes, and a saline touch, while Gavi (Cortese) from Piedmont showcases elegance with bright acidity and delicate stone fruit flavors. The structured Fiano from Campania brings richness with honeyed and nutty undertones, contrasting the lively, green apple and almond notes of Garganega in Soave. Meanwhile, Trebbiano and Grillo offer approachable yet expressive styles, ideal for warm-weather sipping. Whether crisp and refreshing or layered and textured, Italy’s summer whites promise a perfect balance of fruit, acidity, and minerality, making them versatile companions for seasonal cuisine.

CHARDONNAY

Chardonnay is one of the most popular and versatile white grape varieties in the world, known for its ability to reflect the terroir in which it is grown. It produces wines that range from crisp and refreshing to rich and full-bodied, often with flavors of green apple, citrus, tropical fruits, and hints of butter or vanilla when aged in oak. Chardonnay wines can vary significantly based on their climate, with cooler regions producing more acidic, mineral-driven wines, while warmer climates yield fruitier, more opulent styles. Whether unoaked or barrel-aged, Chardonnay is celebrated for its adaptability and wide appeal.

ROSE

Rosé wine is a versatile and refreshing style that bridges the gap between red and white wines. Made from red grape varieties with minimal skin contact, rosé boasts a delicate pink hue and a crisp, fruit-forward profile. Flavors often include red berries like strawberry and raspberry, citrus, and floral notes, with a dry to off-dry finish. Whether from Provence, Italy, Spain, or California, rosé is perfect for warm weather sipping and pairs beautifully with seafood, salads, and light pastas. Its lively acidity and elegant structure make it a go-to for any occasion.
